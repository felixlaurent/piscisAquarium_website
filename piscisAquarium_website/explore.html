FEATURED


Antartica Penguins


Antartica Penguins lives in a world of snow. They are very well

adapted to the cold. Their oily feathers do not allow water

to pass through to their skin. Underneath the outer

feather layer is thick layer of fat which keeps

penguins so warm that they often need to

fluff up their feathers to cool down.



FUN FACT

"Antartica Penguins have poor eyesight on land, but

their vision underwater is incredibly good."

Pacific White-Sided Dolphin


Pacific white-sided dolphins are playful, and highly sociable

marine mammals, known for their unique colors that

bear its name. They are energetic and very active.

They are often seen leaping and somersaulting

in the ocean. This dolphin are also a strong,

and fast swimmer.



FUN FACT

"Pacific White-Sided Dolphin sometimes

"referred to as a hook fin porpoise"

due to its large curved dorsal fin."

FEATURED


Whale Shark


The Whale sharks are not actually whales. It is the largest fish in the world and the largest fish known to have lived on this planet.

Whale sharks can grow up to 12 meters in length, but on average they grow to 5.5-10 meters and weigh 18.7 tonnes. It's the size of a school bus!

Unlike most shark species, its mouth is located at the front of the head. According to National Geographic, whale sharks are meat-loving carnivores,

but they are filter feeders. They cannot bit nor chew . In order to eat, they stretches its impressive large jaw and passively filters everything in its path.



FUN FACT

"Despite being the largest fish in the world, whale sharks feed on some of the smallest creatures in the ocean called zooplankton."
